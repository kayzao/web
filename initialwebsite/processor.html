Processor

Currently, the largest two chip manufacturers for desktop CPUs (Central Processing Unit) are AMD and Intel. Both companies produce industry-leading CPUs that can clock up to 5 Ghz. The higher its clockspeed, the faster it is. However, clockspeed is not the only consideration for a CPU. Overclocking is when you boost your CPU's base speed, given that you have avaiable power supply wattage as well as manageable CPU temperatures. Cores and threads are important because they determine how much work you can do with your CPU.

CPUs also requiere a CPU cooler, so they don't end up killing themselves from the heat they produce, which can reach 100 degrees celsius. There are 2 main types of cpu coolers: air cooling and AIOs. A CPU air cooler is basically a fan and some fins to help dissapate heat, all put on top of a processor. Air coolers are the most basic, as well as the cheapest. An AIO (All in One) is a radiator and a cooler put on top of a processor, connected by flexible piping to contain water. AIOs are more expensive, but normally cool better than air coolers.
