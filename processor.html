Processor

Processors serve as the brain of the computer, executing instructions given to it. Processors are also known as CPUs, which stands for Central Processing Unit. Currently, the two largest chip manufacturers for desktop processors are AMD and Intel. Both companies produce industry-leading CPUs that can clock up to 5 Ghz. The higher its clockspeed, the faster it is. However, clockspeed is not the only consideration for a CPU. Overclocking is when you boost your CPU's base speed, given that you have avaiable power supply wattage as well as manageable CPU temperatures. Cores and threads are important because they determine how much work you can do with your CPU.

AMD CPUs

AMD's current generation of CPUs are the ryzen 5000 lineup, primarily based on the Zen3 architecture. They fit into the B550 and X570 motherboards. Traditionally, AMD processors contain more threads, more cores, and cost less than their Intel counterparts, meaning that they are better for running multiple processes at once. Intel CPUs

Intel's current lineup of CPUs is the 10th generation Core lineup, containing the i3, i5, i7, and i9, all using Intel's new Tiger Lake architecture. Traditionally, Intel processors contain faster cores, meaning they are better for single-core processes, such as playing most games. Intel processors also contain built-in graphics, for if you don't have a dedicated graphics card.

CPUs also requiere a CPU cooler, so they don't end up killing themselves from the heat they produce, which can reach 100 degrees celsius. There are 2 main types of cpu coolers: air cooling and AIOs. A CPU air cooler is basically a fan and some fins to help dissapate heat, all put on top of a processor. Air coolers are the most basic, as well as the cheapest. An AIO (All in One) is a radiator and a cooler put on top of a processor, connected by flexible piping to contain water. AIOs are more expensive, but normally cool better than air coolers.
